## 1. The Fundamentals

Before you can calculate anything, you need to understand **bag yield** — the actual volume of concrete produced from a single bag once water is added and air voids are compacted.

A standard 80 lb bag of premix concrete typically yields about **0.6 ft³ (0.022 yd³)** of cured concrete. That means you’d need roughly **45 bags** to make one cubic yard.  

The basic formula for estimating the number of bags is:

Bags = (Wet Volume × 1.54) ÷ (0.0347 × Sum of Mix Parts)

## 2. Mix Ratios and Material Relationships

Not all concrete is mixed the same way. A **1:2:4 ratio** (cement:sand:aggregate) is standard for structural concrete, while **1:3:6** is common for mass fill or non-load applications.  

The richer the mix (more cement), the more bags you’ll need per cubic yard.

## 7. Field Practices and Pro Tips

Even the best calculations can miss practical realities on site. Field conditions often demand a **5–10% waste buffer** to account for trench irregularities, over-excavation, or absorption losses.  

Understanding true coverage helps prevent shortages mid-pour. The post [Concrete Bag Coverage and Yield](/blog/concrete-bag-coverage-and-yield) provides measured coverage data based on real-world pouring conditions.  

Always:

- Round up to the nearest full bag — never underorder.
- Account for loss during transfer and spillage.
- Adjust for soil compaction around posts or sonotubes.
- Store bags on pallets, off the ground, and protected from moisture.

A small safety margin in bag count saves significant cost and time compared to running out mid-project.
